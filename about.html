Healing Through Connection

Our Mission

At Guardian 4 Heroes, we are dedicated to supporting the mental wellness of our veterans through compassionate and empowering equine-facilitated psychotherapy (EFPT). We provide a safe, therapeutic environment where veterans and first responders can find connection, understanding, and hope.

Our Purpose

Founded by Sara Peterson, a dedicated veteran advocate, our nonprofit strives to create an environment of safety, peace, and healing for our veterans. Our purpose is to provide a space where individuals can rediscover themselves, find strength in community, and heal through the transformative power of horses.

Sara Peterson

A veteran advocate with over 10 years of experience, Sara founded Guardian 4 Heroes to empower veterans and first responders through transformative equine therapy. Her vision creates an environment where healing and growth are possible for every participant.

What Is EFPT?

Equine-Facilitated Psychotherapy Training involves engaging with horses to rebuild confidence, explore boundaries, and enhance self-esteem. Horses, attuned to human emotion, offer a unique and powerful therapeutic experience that fosters personal growth and healing.

Meet the Crew

Our equine partners are at the heart of Guardian 4 Heroes. Each horse brings a unique personality and spirit to our therapy sessions, offering profound connections and opportunities for healing.

Nelly, or Nelly Bell

A 12-year-old Mustang mare, Nelly sometimes pretends she doesn’t want to be brushed or touched. But she'll always come back for snuggles and is constantly aware of your presence, ensuring you're never alone in the arena.

Maverick, or Mav

Our sweet Mav is always there when you’re in need of a hug or snuggles. This 12-year-old rescue loves attention and doesn’t let his disability get in his way, proving that love and bravery conquer all challenges.

Rocket

Rocket is the biggest of our bunch, both in size and personality! He loves to goof around and craves attention. You’d never guess this spirited horse is 26 years old—he’s truly ageless at heart.

Drifter

Drifter may seem a bit aloof at first, but once he gets to know you, his warm personality shines through. At 20 years old, all he wants to do is please, and he absolutely loves brushing time and gentle words.

Veteran Stories

“I’m Craig, a 35-year-old Army veteran who served in Afghanistan. After leaving the Army, I struggled with substance abuse and felt lost. I was hesitant to join Guardian 4 Heroes, even though I knew I needed help. Equine therapy helped me reconnect with myself and others. The horses, and the people here, gave me hope and purpose again.”
